College Success Project Website

Building Stronger College Habits with The Champion's Mind

This project shares how I applied mental performance ideas from sports psychology to my college routine in a practical and realistic way.

Read My Project

About me

I am a college student who is motivated and goal focused, but I used to lose consistency during busy weeks. When I delayed assignments, my stress rose and my work quality dropped.

I wanted a structure that felt realistic for college life and that I could repeat even when classes, practices, and other responsibilities started to pile up.

My goals

I picked goals that matched what I was actually struggling with. I wanted less panic before deadlines and better focus once I began a study session.

Main goal

Follow one short pre study routine before homework so I can start faster and finish work on time.

Specific targets

  • Plan my study tasks at least five days each week
  • Use positive self talk before hard assignments
  • Turn in more work on time over a two week period

Why this matters to me

When I feel in control of my school work, I sleep better, show up with more confidence, and enjoy college more.

The book I selected

The Champion's Mind by Jim Afremow.

Why I picked this book

I picked this book because I have played sports my whole life, especially hockey. The sports mindset in this book immediately connected with me, and I felt I would understand and apply the concepts more easily because of that background.

How it connected to my goals

My goals were about consistency, confidence, and performing under pressure. The book focuses on routines, self talk, and mental preparation, which aligned directly with what I needed for school.

Two ideas I used from the book

Idea one short pre study routine

Before I started homework I did the same quick routine each time.

  1. Two minutes of slow breathing
  2. Write my top three tasks for that session
  3. Start the hardest task for at least five minutes

This helped because starting was usually the hardest part for me. Once I got started with a clear plan, I could stay in the work longer and avoid distractions.

Challenge some days I wanted to skip the routine. What helped I kept a reminder note on my laptop and used a short timer so it felt quick and manageable.

Idea two better self talk during stress

When I caught myself saying I cannot do this, I replaced that thought with short lines like stay calm and do the next step.

This helped me feel less overwhelmed and more focused, especially before quizzes and larger assignments.

Challenge negative thoughts still appeared during stressful weeks. What helped I saved my cue statements in my phone and reviewed them each morning before classes.

Tracking and proof from two weeks

I tracked both strategy use and outcomes for two full weeks. I used a daily checklist in my notes app and a weekly summary every Sunday.

Major takeaways

One small routines work

I did not need a perfect schedule. I needed one repeatable routine that helped me begin.

Two my inner voice matters

The way I talk to myself can either increase pressure or help me take action.

Three tracking keeps me honest

Writing down results each week kept me accountable and made progress visible.

Progress on my goals

I made clear progress toward my goals. I still had some off days, but I improved my consistency, submitted more work on time, and felt more in control during the week.

Advice I would share with other students

  • Pick one habit first and keep it simple
  • Use short cue lines when stress gets loud
  • Track your days in your phone notes so you can see patterns
  • If you miss a day, restart the next day and keep going
